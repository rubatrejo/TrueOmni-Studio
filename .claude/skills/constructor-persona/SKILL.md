# Constructor Persona Skill

## Purpose

This skill guides you through creating comprehensive, actionable buyer personas (customer avatars) for TrueOmni's B2B customer base. These personas help inform marketing messaging, sales strategies, product positioning, and customer experience initiatives.

## TrueOmni Business Context

Before building personas, read `/Users/rubenramirez/Documents/Cursor-Trueomni/README.md` to understand:

- **What TrueOmni Offers:** Interactive touch experiences (kiosks, mobile apps, video walls, digital signage) with vertically integrated hardware + software
- **Target Industries:** Travel & Tourism, Hospitality, Retail
- **Key Products:** IVE (tourism), NEXI (AI-powered hospitality/retail), IRE (retail)
- **Value Propositions:** 24/7 self-service, American-made hardware, 3-year warranty, turnkey installation, AI-powered personalization, industry-specific platforms
- **Customer Pain Points:** Staffing shortages, visitor engagement, revenue generation, data collection, multi-location management, system integration

## Persona Framework

Each persona should include the following components:

### 1. Fictional Profile

- **Name:** Realistic first and last name
- **Photo Description:** Brief visual description (age range, professional appearance, context)
- **Tagline:** One-sentence summary of who they are

### 2. Demographics

- **Age Range:** Specific bracket (e.g., 42-55)
- **Gender:** As relevant
- **Occupation/Title:** Specific role (e.g., Director of Visitor Experience, Hotel General Manager, Retail Operations Director)
- **Company Size:** Number of employees, locations, or revenue bracket
- **Decision-Making Authority:** Budget control, influence level
- **Annual Budget:** Range for technology/capital improvements
- **Geographic Location:** Primary markets (e.g., Mid-sized cities in the US, Resort destinations, Urban retail districts)

### 3. Psychographics

- **Core Values:** What matters most professionally (innovation, efficiency, guest satisfaction, ROI, etc.)
- **Fears:** Professional risks, business threats, career concerns
- **Frustrations:** Current operational pain points
- **Aspirations:** Professional goals, vision for their organization
- **Motivations:** What drives their decision-making

### 4. Core Desire (Primary Category)

Identify the dominant desire driving their decisions:

- **💰 WEALTH** → Revenue growth, cost savings, operational efficiency, ROI, budget optimization
- **🤝 RELATIONSHIPS** → Guest/visitor satisfaction, staff morale, brand reputation, stakeholder alignment
- **🏥 HEALTH** → Business sustainability, competitive positioning, future-proofing, organizational resilience

### 5. Directional Motivation

What drives their action?

- **⬅️ AWAY FROM PAIN** → Solving urgent problems, avoiding risks, fixing inefficiencies, preventing negative outcomes
- **➡️ TOWARD PLEASURE** → Achieving aspirational goals, innovation, competitive advantage, creating positive outcomes

### 6. Pain Points (Minimum 5 Verbatim Phrases)

Capture what they say or think in first-person voice. These should be specific, emotional, and authentic:

**Examples:**

- "We're constantly short-staffed at the front desk and visitors are waiting 15+ minutes just to get basic information."
- "Our current kiosk system looks dated and half the touchscreens don't even work anymore."
- "I need to show ROI on every technology investment, and our current digital signage doesn't give me any analytics."
- "We have five hotel properties and managing content across all of them is a nightmare."
- "Guests expect personalization but we have no way to deliver customized experiences at scale."

### 7. Aspirations (Minimum 5 Verbatim Phrases)

What they want to achieve, in their own words:

**Examples:**

- "I want visitors to walk in and immediately find what they're looking for without needing staff assistance."
- "We should be leveraging AI to upsell room upgrades and spa services automatically during check-in."
- "I'd love to have real-time data showing which attractions and events are most popular with our visitors."
- "Our brand deserves interactive experiences that feel modern and premium, not clunky and outdated."
- "I want technology that just works — no constant IT headaches or vendor support tickets."

### 8. Online Behavior & Information Sources

Where do they learn, research, and make decisions?

- **Industry Publications:** Specific magazines, blogs, newsletters
- **Professional Associations:** Memberships (e.g., DMAI, HSMAI, NRF)
- **Social Media:** LinkedIn groups, Twitter follows, industry influencers
- **Events & Conferences:** Trade shows they attend
- **Peer Networks:** Where they seek recommendations
- **Research Methods:** Google searches, vendor comparisons, case studies, referrals

### 9. Decision-Making Process

- **Who's Involved:** Titles of stakeholders (CEO, CFO, IT Director, Marketing Director, Operations, Board)
- **Timeline:** Typical sales cycle length (weeks/months from awareness to purchase)
- **Evaluation Criteria:** What they prioritize (ROI, warranty, support, ease of use, integration capabilities, aesthetics, references)
- **Budget Approval:** Process for capital expenditures
- **Preferred Engagement:** How they like to be contacted (email, phone, in-person demos, showroom visits)

### 10. Objections & Barriers

Common concerns that might prevent them from buying:

- **Budget Constraints:** "This seems expensive compared to off-the-shelf solutions."
- **Implementation Concerns:** "How long will installation take and will it disrupt operations?"
- **Integration Doubts:** "Will this work with our existing PMS/CRM/booking engine?"
- **Risk Aversion:** "What if the technology becomes obsolete in two years?"
- **Competitive Alternatives:** Other solutions they're considering
- **Internal Politics:** Getting buy-in from stakeholders

## Industry-Specific Persona Archetypes

### Travel & Tourism Personas

**Common Titles:**

- Director of Visitor Experience / Visitor Services
- Tourism Bureau Executive Director
- DMO Marketing Director
- Visitor Center Manager
- Convention & Visitors Bureau CEO
- Tourism Board President

**Typical Pain Points:**

- Staffing shortages at visitor centers
- Inability to track visitor engagement and ROI
- Outdated or non-existent wayfinding solutions
- Need to promote local businesses and events 24/7
- Limited data on visitor behavior and preferences
- Seasonal traffic spikes overwhelming staff
- Multi-location coordination (visitor centers, kiosks at airports, hotels, downtown)

**Typical Aspirations:**

- AI-powered itinerary building for personalized trip planning
- Real-time event and attraction promotion
- Data-driven insights into visitor interests and demographics
- Revenue generation through advertising opportunities for local businesses
- 24/7 self-service information access
- Mobile-friendly experiences that extend beyond physical kiosks

**TrueOmni Solution Fit:** IVE platform, outdoor kiosks, mobile PWA, analytics dashboard

---

### Hospitality Personas

**Common Titles:**

- Hotel General Manager
- Director of Operations
- Director of Guest Experience
- Resort Manager
- Front Office Manager
- Revenue Manager

**Typical Pain Points:**

- Front desk bottlenecks during check-in/check-out
- Missed upsell opportunities (room upgrades, spa, dining)
- Labor costs and staffing challenges
- Inconsistent guest experience across properties
- Limited integration with existing PMS
- No data on guest preferences and behavior
- Difficulty promoting amenities and services effectively

**Typical Aspirations:**

- Automated self-service check-in/check-out
- AI-powered upselling that feels personalized, not pushy
- Seamless PMS integration
- Enhanced guest satisfaction scores
- Operational efficiency and labor cost reduction
- Premium, on-brand guest-facing technology
- Real-time analytics on guest interactions and revenue

**TrueOmni Solution Fit:** NEXI STAY, NEXI DINE, NEXI SHOP, digital signage, PMS integrations

---

### Retail Personas

**Common Titles:**

- Retail Operations Director
- Customer Experience Director
- Store Manager (flagship/corporate locations)
- Director of Digital Strategy
- VP of Retail Innovation
- Multi-location Retail Coordinator

**Typical Pain Points:**

- Difficulty engaging shoppers in-store vs. online competition
- Inconsistent customer experience across locations
- Limited ability to personalize shopping journeys
- No data on in-store customer behavior
- Outdated or ineffective digital signage
- Need for product information and inventory lookup
- Staffing gaps during peak hours

**Typical Aspirations:**

- Interactive shopping experiences that rival online convenience
- Personalized product recommendations and promotions
- Loyalty program integration and digital coupons
- Real-time inventory visibility for customers
- In-store wayfinding and product locators
- Data capture for customer insights and retargeting
- Unified brand experience across all touchpoints

**TrueOmni Solution Fit:** IRE platform, interactive retail kiosks, digital signage, mobile PWA, AI personalization

## Execution Instructions

### Step 1: Understand the Context

- Read `/Users/rubenramirez/Documents/Cursor-Trueomni/README.md` to refresh knowledge of TrueOmni's offerings
- Understand the full scope of products, services, integrations, and value propositions

### Step 2: Clarify the Focus

Use `AskUserQuestion` to gather:

- **Which industry?** Travel & Tourism, Hospitality, or Retail
- **Which product/platform?** IVE, NEXI (Stay/Dine/Shop), IRE, or multi-product buyer
- **Specific use case?** (e.g., hotel self-check-in, visitor center kiosk, retail store wayfinding)
- **Persona scope?** Single persona or multiple personas for different segments
- **Any known details?** Specific titles, company sizes, geographic markets

### Step 3: Build the Persona Interactively

- Start with demographics and role definition
- Identify Core Desire (Wealth / Relationships / Health)
- Determine Direction (Away from Pain / Toward Pleasure)
- Develop pain points collaboratively (ask user for input, supplement with industry knowledge)
- Develop aspirations (what success looks like for this persona)
- Map online behavior and decision-making process
- Identify objections and barriers

### Step 4: Validate & Refine

- Present complete persona to user
- Confirm authenticity (Does this feel like a real person?)
- Adjust based on user feedback
- Ensure all required components are included

### Step 5: Offer Additional Personas

- Ask if user wants to create personas for other segments (e.g., different industries, company sizes, or buyer roles)
- Suggest complementary personas (e.g., economic buyer vs. technical buyer, or end-user vs. decision-maker)
